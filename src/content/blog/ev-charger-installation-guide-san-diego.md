## Why San Diego Homeowners Are Installing EV Chargers

San Diego County has one of the highest rates of electric vehicle adoption in the country. With year-round sunshine and growing infrastructure, more homeowners are choosing to charge at home for convenience, cost savings, and reliability.

## Level 1 vs. Level 2: Understanding Your Options

**Level 1 charging** uses a standard 120V outlet and adds roughly 3–5 miles of range per hour. It works in a pinch but is impractical for most daily drivers.

**Level 2 charging** uses a dedicated 240V circuit and delivers 25–30 miles of range per hour. This is the standard for home installations and what most EV manufacturers recommend.

## Electrical Requirements for Home EV Charger Installation

A Level 2 EV charger typically requires:

- A dedicated 240V, 40–50 amp circuit
- Adequate panel capacity (at least 200 amps recommended)
- Proper wire gauge run from panel to charger location
- GFCI protection per current code requirements

If your panel is older or already near capacity, a panel upgrade or sub-panel installation may be needed before the charger can be installed safely.

## The Installation Process

1. **Assessment** — A licensed electrician evaluates your panel capacity, the distance from panel to charger location, and your vehicle's charging requirements.
2. **Permitting** — Most cities in San Diego County require an electrical permit for EV charger installation. Your electrician should handle this.
3. **Installation** — The dedicated circuit is run, the charger is mounted, and all connections are made to code.
4. **Inspection & Testing** — The city inspector verifies the work, and the electrician confirms the charger operates correctly.

## What Affects the Cost

Every installation is different. Factors that affect pricing include:

- Distance from electrical panel to charger location
- Whether a panel upgrade is needed
- Conduit routing requirements (interior vs. exterior)
- Charger brand and features (hardwired vs. plug-in)
- Permit and inspection fees

## Choosing the Right Electrician

Look for a licensed, bonded, and insured electrician with specific experience in EV charger installations. They should be familiar with local permit requirements and able to assess your panel capacity before starting work.
